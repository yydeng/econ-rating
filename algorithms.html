Ranking Methods

Formal definitions of the scholar-ranking rules used throughout this site.

Index Definition
Existing indices
h-index The largest h such that h papers have at least h citations each
i10-index The number of papers with at least 10 citations
Euclidean index \(\sqrt{\sum_i x_i^2}\), where $x_i$ is the number of citations of paper $i$
Total Citation Total number of citations
Total Publication Total number of publications
Bean-Counting (BC) family
ABC Absolute Bean Counting \( \#\{\text{Top Five}\} \)
ABC_A Absolute Bean Counting \( \#\{\text{Top Five or Top A}\} \)
DBC Discounted Bean Counting \(\#\{\text{Top Five}\} + 0.5 \times \#\{\text{outside Top Five}\}\)
CRBC Citation-Restricted Bean Counting \(\#\{\text{Top Five and with at least 10 citations}\}\)
CEBC Citation-Expanded Bean Counting \(\#\{\text{Top Five or with at least 10 citations}\}\)
TCEBC Tiered Citation-Expanded Bean Counting $\#\{\text{Top Five}\} + 0.5 \times \#\{\text{Top A}\} + 0.5 \times \#\{\text{non-Top-Five/Top-A and with at least 10 citations}\}$
CES family
ACES Asymmetric CES \(\left(\sum_{\text{Top Five}} \sqrt{x_i}\right)^2 + 0.5 \times \sqrt{\sum_{\text{non-Top-Five}} x_i^2}\), where $x_i$ is the number of citations of paper $i$
ABC + CES \(\#\{\text{Top Five}\} + \sqrt{\sum_{\text{non-Top-Five}} x_i^2}\), where $x_i$ is the number of citations of paper $i$
ABC × CES \(\#\{\text{Top Five}\} \times \sqrt{\sum_{\text{non-Top-Five}} x_i^2}\), where $x_i$ is the number of citations of paper $i$

Field Classification

Paper-level field classification and scholar-level primary-field assignment.

We classify publications into 13 economics fields, plus an “Other” category, using a Large Language Model based on each paper's title, keywords, and abstract. Each paper is assigned to the field with the highest predicted probability.

A scholar's primary field is the field with the largest share of their publications. Scholars whose primary field accounts for at least 50% of their publications are treated as single-field scholars, and their primary field is displayed. Scholars whose primary field accounts for less than 50% are labeled as multi-field.
